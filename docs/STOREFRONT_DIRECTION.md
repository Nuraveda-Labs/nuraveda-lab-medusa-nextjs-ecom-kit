# Storefront Direction

## Visual Thesis
A premium retail storefront with editorial warmth, gallery-level restraint, and
enough operational clarity to feel trustworthy. The visual system should feel
closer to considered lifestyle retail than loud discount branding.

## Reference Translation
- Calm hierarchy, precise copy, clean product framing
- Strong merchandising and format-led browsing
- Warm retail personality with display-worthy presentation

These are reference cues only. The site should not visually clone any single brand.

## Sitemap
- `/` — Brand story, featured categories, featured products, trust framing, shop CTA
- `/shop` — Search, category filtering, merchandising-led browsing
- `/shop/[slug]` — Product detail, product cues, related products, add-to-cart
- `/cart` — Basket review
- `/checkout` — Customer form, payment selection, order submission
- `/about` — Brand story, positioning, shopping philosophy, trust framing
- `/contact` — Customer support, order expectations, service windows
- `/faq` — Ordering, delivery, catalog availability, support questions
- `/policies` — Fulfillment, returns/refunds framing, privacy/contact baseline

## Content Priorities
1. Make the storefront feel curated, not bulk-displayed.
2. Keep product language clear and decision-oriented.
3. Use short, premium, client-facing copy.
4. Keep policy and trust information visible but not dominant.
5. Keep checkout language focused; keep order and review language clear.

## Design System
- Backgrounds: warm paper and soft stone instead of near-black panels
- Accent: restrained terracotta, not metallic gold
- Primary contrast: forest green and cream
- Type: editorial serif for section moments, modern sans for all utility copy
- Components: avoid card spam; use larger blocks and stronger spacing rhythm

## Build Sequence
1. Information architecture and trust pages
2. Homepage storytelling and imagery direction
3. Shop taxonomy and richer product merchandising
4. Product detail content depth and related product logic
5. Order follow-up workflow and admin handling
6. SEO, legal, analytics, and launch QA

## Assets to Replace Before Launch
- Final logo/wordmark
- Real product photography or packaging imagery
- Contact details and service hours
- Fulfillment regions / delivery rules
- Final policy text and privacy/contact email
